G10 - 1st Semister
Dovetailed Acrylic Beam

Overview

Egypt's grand challenges are the problems that Egypt faces as the pollution, the useless of arid areas that cover 96% of Egypt and only 4% occupied by the population, the overpopulation (90% of people live in Nile basin), .... etc. The role of the project is to help solve these problems especially the arid areas and urban congestion by exploiting the arid areas and building on them, which will decrease the urban congestion. Of course, the build needs building materials. The building materials these days are conventional and harmful to the environment and humans, so instead of using these materials, there are alternative building materials or as they are been called green materials because they don't pollute the environment. The acrylic was the chosen material to use and build the project's prototype which is a beam as it is one of the most common building constructions. The beam is made from small pieces in the shape of a puzzle and gathered together with the cyanoacrylate as adhesive. To measure the rate of the beam successfully there are some design requirements that must be achieved by the beam like it is must be 50 cm in length and lift 50 N with a maximum deflection of 10mm. After testing the beam achieved the design requirement and record 50 N weight with 2.3 mm deflection.

G10 - 2nd Semister
CARBONIC CELL

Overview

Climate change and global warming are well-known global issues, with greenhouse gas emissions as the primary cause. The only solution is for each country to reduce its contribution to these issues. Egypt's main concern is energy, as the country is heavily reliant on hydrocarbon-based fossil fuels to generate electricity. To assist Egypt in overcoming these challenges, a system should be built at industrial complexes to generate energy and hydrogen fuel while also reducing CO2 emissions from factories, and the electrochemical cell was chosen as a method to implement that system. This cell must meet some design requirements as follow; generating not less than 1.5 volts and producing hydrogen while distinguished by the efficiency of more than 90%, from these requirements, the idea of the prototype was born. An anode is immersed in a container, which is filled with electrolyte solution, and A cathode is immersed in the other, which is filled with distilled water reacted with carbon dioxide gas. Finally, a U-shaped tube with two ends containing a strong electrolyte solution works as a salt bridge that facilitates the flow of ions between oxidation and reduction half-cells. After constructing the cell, the CO2 which is produced from the reaction of baking soda and vinegar added to the cell, where it undergoes some reaction until results are obtained, and the test plan demonstrates how effective the cell is. Based on our findings, CO2 emissions can be beneficial, providing you with energy rather than causing problems.

G11 - 1st Semister
Textile Wastewater Treatment Plant

Overview

Egypt encounters a lot of challenges; these issues are referred to as grand challenges. All of Egypt's Grand challenges are interconnected in some way, and they are naturally influencing one another. In search of services, Egyptians move to major cities near the Nile, which will result in an increase in arid areas, urban congestion, and pollution. The project's primary big problem is the scarcity of clean water resources due to Egypt's reliance on the Nile. The treatment of wastewater from textile industries for agricultural use was chosen as the best course of action. Two physical and chemical treatment techniques are used to accomplish this. The project should be sustainable, durable, and efficient. In addition, the primary goal of the treatment procedure is to improve the water quality while reducing overall costs by improving some wastewater characteristics including TDS, turbidity, and others. The main idea and its results achieved the design requirements. So, the project should have solved the main problem successfully.

G11 - 2nd Semister
SMART FABA BEANS TOWER

Overview

Various Grand Challenges are constantly facing Egypt, some of which are climate change, pollution, lack of sufficient industrial base, and lack of using scientific and technological environment for all. This project works to participate in solving these challenges by finding innovative solutions to agricultural issues faced by the country. This project aimed to harness technology to create a sustainable feedback system on aeroponic vertical farming for increasing the crop yield of fava beans in Egypt. The design requirements for the project were having a stable temperature range from 18 to 25 degrees Celsius, humidity range from 70% to 75%, and total dissolved solids (TDS) of from 800 to 1500 ppm. A prototype was developed, consisting of a tower with three containers stacked on top of one another. The first container collected water, the second analyzed and treated it using Arduino sensors and actions, and the third collected usable treated water, which was then pumped upwards to the plant using a water pump. The purpose of the study was to increase the crop yield of fava beans, which was achieved through the efficient working of the project, meeting the design requirements. The results showed that the system could effectively manage temperature, humidity, and TDS levels, resulting in increased crop yield. The sustainable feedback system could help address the agricultural challenges in Egypt and promote sustainable agriculture practices.
